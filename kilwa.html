Kilwa Kisiwani

Ancient ruins of a powerful Swahili trade city

About Kilwa Kisiwani

Kilwa Kisiwani was a major port on the Swahili coast and a center of trade from the 9th to 16th centuries. The ruins include a mosque, palace, and fort that show the wealth and influence of this city-state. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

How to Reach

Kilwa Kisiwani is located off the southern coast of Tanzania. You can travel to Kilwa Masoko by road from Dar es Salaam (about 5–6 hours), then take a short boat ride to the island.
